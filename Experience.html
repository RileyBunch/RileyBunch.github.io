Tango Flight

In my senior year of high school, the engineering teacher Mr. Dan Weyant started a program called Tango Flight, only a select few were chosen to participate in the experimental class. This class was like no other high school class in the nation in the sense that, we would be building and flying an FAA approved two-seated airplane. In this project I quickly stepped up by leading a small group of students and was trusted with unique responsibilities. For example, when it came time to install the engine into the front of the Van's Aircraft I was trusted with solving a fitment problem. It seemed impossible trying to configure the engine into the small engine bay, and I was trusted and instructed to modify the engine by removing material on a redundant engine mount for proper fitment. By the end of the year, I got to experience flight in the very project we had been working so diligently on; I felt a sense of accomplishment from collaborative teamwork.
